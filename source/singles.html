After the release of his critically acclaimed album "Blonde" in 2016, Frank Ocean has released a handful of singles that have further cemented his reputation as one of the most innovative and boundary-pushing artists in music.

In 2017, Ocean released "Lens," a dreamy track that features introspective lyrics about relationships and self-discovery. The song was released in two versions, one featuring Travis Scott and the other with just Ocean's vocals.

Later that year, Ocean released "Provider," a laid-back and introspective track that features a dreamy instrumental and poignant lyrics about fame and identity. The song was accompanied by a lyric video that featured Ocean playing with vintage camcorders and creating homemade visuals.

In 2018, Ocean released "Moon River," a cover of the classic song from the film "Breakfast at Tiffany's." The stripped-down rendition features Ocean's soulful vocals and acoustic guitar, showcasing his ability to breathe new life into classic songs.

In 2019, Ocean released "DHL," a pulsating track that features frank lyrics about love and drug use. The song was released alongside a music video that features grainy footage of Ocean singing and dancing in front of a green screen.

Later that year, Ocean released "In My Room," a thumping track that features a hypnotic beat and Ocean's trademark falsetto vocals. The song was accompanied by a music video that features psychedelic visuals and images of Ocean dancing in a dimly lit room.

Overall, Frank Ocean's singles after "Blonde" continue to showcase his ability to push the boundaries of modern music and experiment with different genres and production styles. His unique voice and raw emotion continue to captivate audiences, and his singles stand as a testament to his lasting impact on the music industry.
